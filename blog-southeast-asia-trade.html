ASEAN-India Trade Overview

The ASEAN-India Free Trade Agreement (AIFTA) has created a powerful trade corridor connecting India to Southeast Asia's 700 million consumers. Bilateral trade crossed $130 billion in 2025, with significant untapped potential in manufacturing inputs and consumer goods.

India's "Act East Policy" has strengthened ties with the region, creating opportunities for exporters of chemicals, textiles, and engineering products.

As global supply chains diversify from China ("China+1" strategy), Southeast Asian manufacturers are sourcing more inputs from India, creating sustained B2B export demand.

Vietnam is the fastest-growing economy in ASEAN and a major manufacturing hub for global brands. With Samsung, Apple, and other giants manufacturing there, demand for components and raw materials is immense.

Indonesia Opportunities

Indonesia is ASEAN's largest economy with 280 million consumers and a rapidly growing middle class. The archipelago nation imports heavily in food, textiles, and industrial goods.

Market Drivers

  • Population: 4th largest in the world, young demographics
  • Urbanization: 56% urban, growing consumption
  • Infrastructure: $430 billion spending planned to 2030
  • Digital Economy: Fastest growing in region

üìã Entry Requirements

Indonesia requires SNI (Indonesian National Standard) certification for many products. Register with BPOM for food and pharmaceuticals. Halal certification is mandatory for food products targeting Muslim consumers.

Thailand Market Guide

Thailand is a manufacturing powerhouse known as the "Detroit of Asia" for automotive and the regional hub for electronics. It serves as gateway to Indochina markets.

Export Opportunities

  • Auto Components: Major auto manufacturing base
  • Chemicals: Industrial chemicals demand
  • Gems & Jewelry: Global trading hub
  • Food Ingredients: Processing industry inputs

FTA Benefits & Duties

The ASEAN-India FTA provides significant tariff benefits:

1

Normal Track (NT)

Duties reduced to 0% on 80% of tariff lines over scheduled period.

2

Sensitive Track (ST)

Partial reduction on sensitive products like agriculture and textiles.

3

Certificate of Origin

Form AI required to claim preferential rates. Obtain from authorized agencies.

4

Rules of Origin

Products must meet value addition criteria (typically 35-40% local content).

Logistics & Infrastructure

Option 1

Sea Freight

Most economical. Chennai/Kolkata to Singapore/Vietnam in 7-10 days. Use for bulk cargo.

Option 2

Air Freight

Premium products or urgent shipments. Delhi/Mumbai to Bangkok/Jakarta in 4-6 hours.

Option 3

Land Route

India-Myanmar-Thailand corridor developing. Best for Northeast India exporters.
