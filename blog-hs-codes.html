Learn how to correctly classify your products using the Harmonized System to ensure accurate customs duties and smooth clearance.

What are HS Codes?

The Harmonized System (HS) is an internationally standardized system of names and numbers for classifying traded products. Developed by the World Customs Organization (WCO), it's used by over 200 countries.

HS codes determine the customs duty rates applicable to your products, regulatory requirements, and eligibility for trade agreement benefits. Wrong classification can lead to:

  • Overpaying or underpaying duties
  • Shipment delays and customs holds
  • Penalties for incorrect classification
  • Loss of FTA preferential rates

The HS system covers over 5,000 commodity groups identified by 6-digit codes. Countries add additional digits for national classification.

Understanding HS Code Structure

HS codes follow a hierarchical structure:

1-2

Chapter (2 digits)

Broad product category. Example: 52 = Cotton

3-4

Heading (4 digits)

More specific grouping. Example: 5208 = Woven cotton fabrics

5-6

Subheading (6 digits)

Detailed classification. Example: 520812 = Plain weave cotton, over 100g/m¬≤

7-8

National Tariff (8 digits)

Country-specific. Example: 52081290 = India-specific classification

Finding the Correct HS Code

Step-by-Step Approach

Step 1

Identify Product Characteristics

Material composition, function, end-use, manufacturing process

Step 2

Find the Chapter

Determine broad category (Chapters 1-97)

Step 3

Narrow to Heading

Find 4-digit heading within the chapter

Step 4

Determine Subheading

Select 6-digit code based on specific characteristics

Step 5

Add National Digits

Apply country-specific 8-digit tariff code

‚ùå

Ignoring End-Use
