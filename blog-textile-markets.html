Market Overview

India's textile industry is one of the largest in the world, contributing significantly to the country's GDP and employment. In 2025-26, the textile export sector is projected to reach $50 billion, driven by growing global demand for sustainable and quality fabrics.

The Indian textile export sector benefits from a complete value chain‚Äîfrom raw cotton cultivation to finished garments. This integrated approach gives Indian exporters a competitive edge in the global marketplace.

India ranks as the world's 2nd largest textile exporter, accounting for 4.6% of global textile trade. The sector employs over 45 million workers directly.

Top 10 Export Markets for 2026

1. United States of America

The USA remains India's largest textile export destination, importing over $8 billion worth of textiles annually. Key products include cotton apparel, home textiles, and technical textiles. The US market particularly values:

  • Organic cotton products
  • Sustainable and eco-friendly fabrics
  • Ready-made garments (RMG)

2. European Union

EU countries collectively import approximately $6 billion in Indian textiles. Germany, UK, France, and Italy are the major buyers, with strong demand for luxury fabrics and designer textiles.

3. United Arab Emirates

UAE serves as both a direct market and a re-export hub to Africa and other Middle Eastern countries. The Dubai textile market is particularly significant for Indian exporters of traditional fabrics.

4. Bangladesh

Though known for its garment industry, Bangladesh imports significant quantities of raw materials and fabrics from India for its manufacturing sector.

5. United Kingdom

Post-Brexit, the UK has emerged as a standalone major market with the India-UK FTA negotiations promising enhanced market access for Indian textiles.

6-10: Emerging Markets

The remaining top markets include Sri Lanka, Australia, Canada, Spain, and Japan. These markets show strong growth potential, especially for specialized textiles and sustainable products.

Emerging Opportunities

Beyond traditional markets, several regions present exciting growth opportunities:

Africa

African nations are increasingly importing Indian textiles. Countries like Kenya, South Africa, Ethiopia, and Nigeria show strong demand for affordable quality fabrics. Read our detailed analysis on Africa's growing import demand.

Latin America

Brazil, Mexico, and Colombia are emerging as significant buyers of Indian textiles, particularly technical textiles and cotton fabrics.

Southeast Asia

Vietnam, Indonesia, and Thailand are growing markets, often serving as manufacturing hubs that require quality fabric inputs.
